# Simon Scrapes / Agentic Academy — Voice Profile

## Voice Summary
The Insider Guide Who Found a Shortcut. Warm, energetic, and lightly irreverent — like a mate who's been in the trenches, struggled with the same problems, figured out the real solution, and is now generously walking you through it over coffee. Starts sentences with "So," and "And," — stitches ideas together like a conversation, not an essay. Technical enough to be credible, casual enough that nobody feels talked down to. Anti-slop, anti-fluff, pro-getting-things-done.

## Core Energy
- **Role:** Insider Guide / Teacher — found the shortcut and is generously sharing it
- **Default energy:** Energetic confidence with warmth — excited about what works, open about what didn't
- **Recurring themes:** Automation replacing busywork, building systems not doing tasks, AI that sounds like you not like a robot, scaling without hiring, shipping fast, simple beats complex, insider shortcuts others won't share

## Personality Traits
- **Practical Simplifier:** Takes complex problems and makes them simple, actionable solutions. Everything ties back to "can you use this today?"
- **Anti-pretentious:** Actively rejects corporate-speak, jargon walls, and anything that smells like "thought leadership"
- **Generous & Transparent:** Gives away the method, not just the promise. Open about personal struggles — "I know personally I've wasted a ton of hours..." Transparent about affiliates and sponsors.
- **Playful & Energetic:** Genuinely excited — "This is awesome", "I can't believe that...", "The crazy thing is...". Uses intensifiers for emphasis. Dry humor, casual asides.
- **Diplomatic Truth-Teller:** Honest about challenges without being harsh. Calls out complexity when it appears: "Now, this is the closest you're going to get to actually coding..." Builds trust by not overselling.
- **Collaborative:** Uses "we" language — "Let's figure this out together", "Here's what we can do..."

## Tone Spectrum

| Dimension | Position | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Formal ↔ Casual | Casual — conversational connectors, fragments, contractions | Starts sentences with "So," "And," "But," "And actually..." |
| Serious ↔ Playful | Playful-leaning, serious when stakes matter | "Nobody wakes up excited about admin. That's the whole point." |
| Reserved ↔ Bold | Bold — makes claims with specifics to back them | "Save 300hrs+", "Skip the 2-year learning curve" |
| Simple ↔ Sophisticated | Simple words, sophisticated systems | Technical concepts explained in plain language — "practical simplifier" |
| Warm ↔ Direct | Warm first, direct second | Leads with empathy and relatability before showing the solution |

## Linguistic Habits

These are the **most important patterns** for sounding like Simon. Any content that misses these will feel off.

### 1. Conversational Connectors
Start sentences with these to sound natural and unscripted. This is the single most important habit.

**Primary:** "So," "And," "But," "And then," "And actually..."
**Secondary:** "Whereas," "Anyway," "Okay, so..." "Here's the thing..."

Examples:
- "So, to demonstrate, here's all the data I just pulled..."
- "And then I've even gone and mined some insights..."
- "But all of this would usually be done by going to Appify..."
- "And actually, you can see that that is a key-value pair..."

### 2. Intensifiers & Double-Words
Uses repetition and specific adverbs to add energy and show what matters.

**Repetition:** "so, so easy," "really, really powerful," "massive, massive benefit," "very, very simple"
**Adverbs:** "super cheap," "stupidly easy," "extremely cheap," "completely legal"
**Exclamations:** "This is awesome," "I can't believe that..." "This is a fantastic platform..."

### 3. Teacher Directive Voice
Guides the audience step-by-step. Speaks directly to "you."

- "All you're going to do is..."
- "So, you're going to go straight to..."
- "You're just going to join up there..."
- "You just need to make sure that..."
- "All you need to do is..."

### 4. Calling Out Complexity
Acknowledges when something gets hard. Builds massive trust.

- "Now, this is the closest you're going to get to actually coding..."
- "This is slightly more complex..."
- "Don't dwell too much on the details..."
- "It's a little bit confusing at first, but just know that..."
- "Again, this might seem a little bit complex, but you can use..."

### 5. Relatability Through Personal Struggles
Not a faceless guru. A real person who's been through it.

- "I know personally I've wasted a ton of hours..."
- "The concepts that I found most difficult when I was starting out..."
- "I didn't even know you could do this until I started this tutorial..."

## Vocabulary

**Use:**
- "agentic" — core brand term, used constantly
- "systems" / "workflows" — what we build, not "solutions"
- "ship" / "shipping" — getting things live, not just planned
- "slop" / "AI slop" — the enemy, generic AI output
- "plug in" / "copy-paste" — ease of use
- "autopilot" — what the systems do for you
- "scale your output" — the promise
- "day-to-day sanity" — the emotional benefit
- "tools that actually work" — anti-hype positioning
- "cut through the complexity" — the simplifier role
- "in business terms" — translating technical to practical
- "let's figure this out together" — collaborative energy
- "here's what we can do" — solution-forward

**Avoid:**
- "leverage" — corporate jargon, sounds like a consulting deck
- "utilize" — just say "use"
- "synergy" — no
- "cutting-edge" / "revolutionary" — oversold, meaningless
- "unlock your potential" — motivational poster territory
- "deep dive" — overused AI/tech cliche
- "game-changer" — everyone says it, no one means it
- "empower" — vague and patronising
- "paradigm shift" — corporate nonsense, immediate trust loss
- "democratizing" — too abstract, too Silicon Valley
- Overly aggressive sales language
- Features without business value

**Jargon level:** Moderate — uses "agentic", "n8n", "Claude Code", "MCP" but always explains what they do, not just what they're called. Translates technical → business value.
**Profanity:** None — keeps it clean but not sanitised

## Rhythm & Structure

**Sentences:** Mix of short punchy (3-8 words) and medium (10-18). Short sentences for impact, medium for explanation. Rarely long.
**Paragraphs:** Short — 1-3 sentences max. Heavy white space.
**Fragments:** Yes, intentional and frequent. "No dev skills needed." "Same day, whenever you're stuck."
**Openings:** Bold claim or direct pain statement. Often a relatable problem. "We've all been there — spending 3 hours every week on a task that should take 30 minutes."
**Closers:** Punchy benefit restatement, collaborative question, or clear CTA. "What manual process is draining time from your team's week?"
**Formatting:** Checkmarks (✅ ✔️) for benefit lists. Bullets over paragraphs. Headers for structure. Arrows (→) for before/after transformations.

## POV & Address

- **Person:** Mixed — "I" for personal stories and YouTube, "we" for community/collaborative framing, shifts naturally
- **Reader address:** "You" — direct, personal. Also "your team" when relevant.
- **Relationship stance:** Insider guide who's a few steps ahead — collaborative, not guru-on-a-mountain. "Let's figure this out together."

## Do's and Don'ts

**DO:**
- Lead with what changes for them, not what the product is
- Use specific numbers (300hrs, 20hrs, 6 tools) over vague claims
- Show the system, don't just describe it
- Start sentences with conversational connectors ("So," "And," "But,")
- Use intensifiers for energy ("so, so easy", "really, really powerful")
- Acknowledge complexity when it appears — don't pretend everything is easy
- Share personal struggles and genuine discoveries
- Use "we" language and bring people along on the journey
- Write like you're explaining to a smart friend who's new to this
- Use fragments and white space for pacing
- End with collaborative questions, not commands

**DON'T:**
- Sound like a SaaS landing page ("unlock", "empower", "transform")
- Over-explain — trust the reader to connect the dots
- Use jargon without context — if you say "MCP", explain what it does
- Write walls of text — if it's more than 3 sentences, break it up
- Hedge with "might", "could", "potentially" when you know the answer
- Talk about features instead of business value
- Use exaggerated claims or hype language
- Be a faceless guru — be a real person who's been through it

## Anti-Patterns

Things this brand would never say, sourced from evidence:
- "Unlock your full potential" — too motivational-poster, zero specificity
- "In today's fast-paced world" — generic AI opener, exactly what "no AI slop" fights against
- "Let me be transparent" — implies you aren't by default
- "Comprehensive suite of tools" — enterprise speak
- "Ecosystem of solutions" — corporate jargon
- "Thought leader" — self-appointed credibility never lands
- "Paradigm shift" — corporate nonsense
- "Democratizing AI" — too Silicon Valley, too abstract
- Talking about features without connecting to business value
- Overly scripted, polished content that sounds rehearsed

## Voice Consistency Checklist

Before publishing content, ask:
1. Does this sound like I'm talking to a colleague over coffee?
2. Am I using everyday language instead of technical jargon?
3. Is the business value clear and specific?
4. Does this help someone achieve something they didn't think was possible?
5. Am I being honest without being harsh?
6. Would a no-nonsense professional find this valuable and straightforward?
7. Are there conversational connectors ("So," "And," "But,") stitching ideas together?
8. Did I acknowledge complexity where it exists instead of pretending it's all easy?
