# Simon Scrapes / Agentic Academy — Ideal Customer Profile

## Who They Are

**In one sentence:** Business owners making enough revenue to feel the pain of manual work, but not enough to justify hiring a team to fix it.

**Situation:** Running a business where they're doing everything — content, outreach, ops, admin — and the repetitive work is eating their time. They know AI can help but haven't figured out how to make it actually work for their business (not just ChatGPT for rewording emails).
**Identity:** Self-starters. Resourceful. They built their business themselves and want to keep that control, but the manual work is becoming the bottleneck.
**Role:** Founder/operator doing 80% of the work themselves. Might have a VA or small team, but nothing that removes the admin burden.
**Stage:** Intermediate — they've tried AI tools, maybe automated a few things, but don't have a system. Still doing most things manually.

## Primary Pain

**The problem:** They're spending hours every day on repetitive work across 6+ tools — content creation, outreach, admin, ops — and it's the thing stopping them from growing.
**What it costs them:** 4+ hours daily on work that doesn't directly grow the business. Opportunity cost of projects they never start. Mental load of context-switching across tools.
**How they describe it:** "I know I should automate this but I don't know where to start." "I've been meaning to build that workflow for months." "I can't hire right now, so I just do it myself."

**Secondary pains:**
- AI output sounds robotic — they've tried ChatGPT but the content doesn't sound like them
- They see others automating and feel behind, but the learning curve feels too steep
- When they do try to build automations, they get stuck and have no one to ask

## What They've Tried

| Alternative | Why it didn't work | What they learned |
|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| ChatGPT / generic AI tools | Output sounds generic — "AI slop." Requires constant prompting and editing. Doesn't know their brand. | AI can help but needs structure and context to be useful |
| YouTube tutorials | Overwhelming, fragmented, often outdated. Hard to piece together a complete system. | They want a guided path, not a scavenger hunt |
| Hiring a VA or freelancer | Expensive, hard to manage, quality inconsistent. Still requires their time to train and review. | They want the capability, not another person to manage |
| Zapier / Make (simple automations) | Can handle simple triggers but can't do complex, multi-step, context-aware workflows. Felt limited. | They need something more powerful — agentic, not just automated |
| Doing nothing | The work piles up. They max out on hours and hit a ceiling. | The problem only gets worse as they grow |

**Sophistication level:** Intermediate — tried 1-3 things, developing skepticism about silver-bullet promises, but still actively looking for a real solution.

## Desired Outcome

**What "after" looks like:** Their business runs systems that handle the repetitive work — content, outreach, ops, admin — in their voice, at their quality standard. They've doubled their output without hiring. The workflows they kept "meaning to build" are live and running.
**What they'd tell a friend:** "I set up these agentic systems and they just... handle it. Content goes out, leads get followed up, admin gets done. And it actually sounds like me."
**The deeper motivation:** Freedom. Not freedom from their business — freedom to do the parts of their business they actually enjoy. Strategy, creative work, client relationships. Not formatting spreadsheets and writing the same email for the 50th time.

## Objections

| Objection | What's really behind it | How to address |
|-----------|------------------------|----------------|
| "I'm not technical enough" | Fear of failure. They've tried dev tools before and bounced off. | "No dev skills needed" — show non-technical people using it. Claude Code course goes from zero to master. |
| "AI output always sounds generic" | They've been burned by ChatGPT slop. Don't trust AI to represent their brand. | "Everything runs in your brand voice. No AI slop." Show real before/after of voice-matched output. |
| "I don't have time to learn another tool" | They're already overwhelmed. Adding complexity feels worse. | "Copy-paste systems — plug them in and they work today." Show the 20-minute version, not the 20-hour course. |
| "How is this different from Zapier/Make?" | They've seen automation promises before. | "This is agentic, not just automated." Show a system making decisions, not just moving data. |
| "Can I actually make money selling this?" | They're interested in the agency angle but unsure about the market. | Agency Launchpad — pricing, proposals, blueprints. Show others doing it. |

## Their Language

**Words they use for the problem:**
- "I'm drowning in admin" — overwhelm, not just inconvenience
- "I keep meaning to set that up" — procrastination from not knowing how
- "I can't hire right now" — budget constraint as permission to not solve it
- "It sounds like AI wrote it" — the slop problem

**Words they use for the desired outcome:**
- "Running on autopilot" — hands-off systems
- "Doubled my output" — concrete, measurable
- "It sounds like me" — voice quality signal

**Words that would turn them off:**
- "Enterprise-grade" — they're not enterprise, feels exclusionary
- "Democratizing AI" — too abstract, too Silicon Valley
- "Paradigm shift" — corporate nonsense, immediate trust loss

**Jargon level:** Light to moderate — they know "automation", "AI", "workflows". They're learning "agentic", "Claude Code", "n8n". They don't know (and don't care about) "MCP servers", "API endpoints", "webhook payloads" unless it's explained in context.

## Where They Hang Out

|-----------------|----------------|---------------|
| YouTube | Learning — watching tutorials, comparing tools, following creators | Primary discovery channel. They search for solutions here first. |
| Skool | Community — asking questions, sharing wins, getting unstuck | Where conversion happens. Peer proof + same-day support. |

**People they trust:** Independent creators who build in public, other founders sharing real results (not influencers selling dreams). Prefer practitioners over theorists.

## Buying Triggers

What pushes them from "considering" to "buying":
- Seeing a specific system demo that solves their exact problem ("that's my workflow, but automated")
- Realising the time they've wasted manually doing something that could be handled in minutes
- A competitor or peer automating and getting visible results
- Hitting a growth ceiling where manual work is clearly the bottleneck
- Finding a community where people like them are already doing it (social proof + support)

## Secondary Segments

### Segment: Agency Builders
- **Who:** People who want to sell agentic workflow services to other businesses
- **Key difference from primary:** They're not just solving their own problem — they want to build a business around solving other people's problems. More interested in pricing, proposals, client management.
- **Priority:** High — strong revenue potential, highly engaged in community

### Segment: Technical Explorers
- **Who:** Developers or technically-minded people who want to go deeper into Claude Code and n8n
- **Key difference from primary:** Less need for hand-holding, more interested in advanced techniques, custom builds, and edge cases
- **Priority:** Medium — smaller segment but high engagement and word-of-mouth potential
