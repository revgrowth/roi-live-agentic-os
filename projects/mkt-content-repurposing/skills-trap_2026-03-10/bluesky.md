## Post 1: Considered Take

Noticing a pattern in Claude Code skill setups:

The people getting consistent, quality output have 20-30 curated skills. The people frustrated by "bad skills" have 100+.

Claude has a 15K char budget for its skills list. More installed = noisier menu = worse picks. Counterintuitive but measurable.

---

## Post 2: Deeper Layer

The real value of a Claude Code skill isn't in the SKILL.md file.

It's in the reference files you add around it — brand voice, examples, audience data, rules.

The skill is just the process. The references are the context. Most people skip the references entirely and wonder why the output feels generic.

---

## Post 3: Question

Real question for anyone building Claude Code skills:

Do you separate your process (what Claude does) from your knowledge (brand context, examples, rules)?

Or is everything in one SKILL.md file?

Curious because the difference in output quality is massive and it's the thing I see even experienced builders still getting wrong.
