# ROI.LIVE — Positioning

## Primary Angle

**Name:** The Unified Strategy + Execution Operator
**Statement:** ROI.LIVE is the fractional CMO who also owns the agency executing against the plan, backed by 18 years of CRM-verified outcome data across 55+ industries that no SaaS tool and no competitor holds.
**Psychology:** Founders in the $1-10M band have already tried fragmented solutions — a consulting CMO + a separate agency — and watched the strategy and execution drift apart because they had separate owners. Jason closes the gap structurally (same person owns both) and credibly (proprietary data a founder can interrogate by client name and date). That's the moat, stated plainly.
**Headline direction:** "The fractional CMO that also runs the team. 18 years of client data. Every claim has a date."
**Best for:** Founders and business owners at $1-3M looking to scale to $5-10M+ across e-commerce, local service, and B2B who've already been burned by the consultant-hands-off-to-agency pattern — or who anticipate it and want to skip the step.

## Market Assessment

**Sophistication:** Stage 4 — The audience has seen the dominant claims (every CMO has "strategy," every agency has "performance"). They're now filtering for *mechanism* and *proof*, not more claims. This is why specific numbers and client-named evidence dominate the brand.
**Transformation:** The founder stops carrying the coordination overhead between a strategy voice and an execution team. Revenue growth stops drifting as plans get handed off and half-implemented. The data the founder trusts for decision-making is grounded in their own CRM and in Jason's cross-industry library — not in SaaS benchmarks that have never seen their pipeline.
**Mechanism:** One operator holds the CMO seat *and* owns the delivery team. The plan and the execution never drift apart because they don't have separate owners. On top of that, proprietary performance data from 55+ industries is brought to bear on every strategic decision — lead-to-job conversion at specific price points, offer tests at specific AOVs, channel ROAS at specific stages. Decisions are made with math, not opinions.
**Primary alternative:** Hire a solo fractional CMO (all strategy, no execution control) *and* hire a performance agency separately (execution without strategic ownership). The alternative fails because the two parties don't share a plan, don't share accountability for revenue, and don't share the data loop.

**Saturated claims (avoid):**
- "Performance marketing that drives results"
- "Data-driven strategy"

- "Growth-focused CMO services"
- Any version of "we do strategy AND execution" without the structural explanation of *why* that works (one owner, not two vendors)

**White space identified:**
- The specific structural claim: one operator holds both seats, so the plan and the execution share an owner. Most competitors make a version of this claim functionally ("we handle it end-to-end") but Jason is unusual in naming it as the *solution to a named failure mode* other founders have already experienced.
- CRM-verified, client-name-and-date data as the proof layer. Most competitors cite industry benchmarks. Jason cites his own pipeline.
- "Operator, not pundit" — Jason running other companies alongside the agency (Constellation Capital, ROI.Contractors) as a credibility anchor. This is rarely explicit in the category.

## All Angles Explored

### Angle 1: The Unified Strategy + Execution Operator ★ selected
- Statement: ROI.LIVE is the fractional CMO who also owns the agency executing against the plan, eliminating the strategy-execution drift that breaks most founder engagements.
- Psychology: Solves a pain the target audience has already lived through. Structurally named solution beats feature-list promises.
- Headline: "The fractional CMO that also runs the team. 18 years of client data. Every claim has a date."
- Best for: $1-10M founders who've tried fragmented models and want one accountability line.

### Angle 2: The Receipt Keeper
- Statement: Every strategic recommendation Jason makes is backed by a specific client, a specific date, and a specific revenue number — not industry benchmarks, not SaaS averages, not opinions.
- Psychology: Differentiates against the sea of CMOs and agencies making directional claims. Inoculates against "all marketing is opinions" skepticism.
- Headline: "18 years. 55+ industries. Every claim has a receipt."
- Best for: Founders who've been burned by directional advice that didn't survive contact with their funnel.

### Angle 3: The Operator, Not the Pundit
- Statement: Jason runs Constellation Capital and ROI.Contractors alongside ROI.LIVE — signing payroll, making offers, losing deals every week. Strategy comes from current operational reality, not from books.
- Psychology: Credibility through parallel skin-in-the-game. Differentiates from consultants who haven't run a P&L in a decade.
- Headline: "The CMO who's also the operator. That's not a title. That's payroll this Friday."
- Best for: Founders who sniff out advisor tourists inside thirty minutes.

### Angle 4: The Quiet Part Out Loud
- Statement: Most founders have already tried the obvious playbook. ROI.LIVE tells them what nobody else will — the offer is wrong, the pricing is wrong, the team is pointing at the wrong metric.
- Psychology: Provocative pull for founders fatigued by polite advice. Self-selects the right client (the ones who want pushback) and repels the wrong ones (the ones who don't).
- Headline: "Your agency won't say this. Your last fractional CMO wouldn't say this. ROI.LIVE says it in the first call."
- Best for: Second- and third-hire engagements — founders who've already tried one or two solutions.

### Angle 5: The Cross-Industry Pattern-Finder
- Statement: Principles that work in e-commerce transfer to HVAC. What fixed a premium DTC brand's email revenue fixes a pool builder's lead-to-job rate. 55 industries of pattern recognition turn every engagement into a shortcut.
- Psychology: Turns perceived generalist weakness (not industry-specialized) into a specific advantage (cross-pollination). Works especially for verticals that think they're unique.
- Headline: "What worked for an oracle deck brand fixed a pool company's funnel. The math doesn't care about your vertical."
- Best for: Founders in "niche" verticals who assume their industry is too specific for outside help.

## Selection Rationale

Angle 1 (Unified Strategy + Execution Operator) wins as primary for three reasons. First, it diagnoses a named failure mode the target audience has already experienced, which makes the promise immediately credible — this is how Jason opened the differentiation answer during /start-here, and it's the opening move on the ROI.LIVE homepage. Second, it bundles the other angles underneath: "unified operator" implies the receipts (Angle 2) and the operator credibility (Angle 3) without requiring them to be stated upfront. Third, it aligns with the current sales motion — most founders who sign stay on retainer past the initial engagement, which suggests the proof point is the integrated delivery, not the upstream strategy alone.

Angles 2, 3, and 4 should be used as *secondary hooks* inside ads, LinkedIn content, and email openers. Angle 5 is the right angle when pitching into verticals where the founder has been told "nobody outside our industry understands us" — particularly useful for local service and niche e-commerce.
