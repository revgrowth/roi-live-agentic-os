# Coastal Carolina Comfort — AC Repair SEO/AEO Master Strategy
## Summerville + Charleston

---

## 1. Executive summary

**The situation.** Coastal Carolina Comfort (CCC) owns one ranking web asset, **coastalcarolinahvac.com**, and one local lead engine, a Summerville Google Business Profile branded Coastal Carolina Comfort. Both are the only properties in scope for this engagement. Rankings on the site peaked Feb–Mar 2026 and have slipped through April on most tracked terms. The site has meaningful organic presence in Charleston HVAC SERPs but almost nothing in Summerville — the #1 named geography in the ICP.

**Scope decisions already locked:**

1. **One domain.** All work happens on coastalcarolinahvac.com. No migration, no parallel build on any other domain.
2. **One GBP.** A single Summerville GBP, staying branded Coastal Carolina Comfort. No new listings for Charleston metro or Myrtle Beach as part of this engagement.
3. **Brand hold.** The website and GBP continue to read Coastal Carolina Comfort throughout peak season. Logo has been swapped on the site; everything else (NAP, copy, GBP name, schema, reviews) stays as-is to preserve the "carolina heating and air" branded-rank equity and avoid a Google re-evaluation of the entity.

**What the data shows.**
- **Reachable in 90 days:** 9 Charleston keywords sitting positions 20–45 that can move to page 1 with on-page fixes, GBP work, and link equity.
- **Bleeding now:** 8 keywords that dropped 10–40 positions since March. Investigate before building new.
- **Blind spot:** Summerville. No tracked keywords. Zero ranking URLs. The #1 named geo in the ICP is invisible in search. This is the biggest single growth lever.
- **AEO window open:** AI Overviews appear on 8+ target keywords. Short, factual, schema-wrapped answer blocks are undervalued by competitors in this market.

**Strategy in one sentence.** On coastalcarolinahvac.com, consolidate cannibalized URLs, fix and AEO-optimize the Charleston cluster, build a Summerville hub and AC-repair spoke cluster from scratch, and rebuild the Summerville GBP as the single local-lead engine — in that order.

**Pattern:** every decline started between March 15 and April 10. That window maps directly to **Google's Information Gain algorithm update (late March 2026)**. The most likely causes, in order of probability:
1. **Information Gain update** *(primary)* — the update ranks pages by how much *new* information they add beyond what the top SERP results already contain. The declining pages all share the vendor-CMS template URL pattern (`-617782`, `-617779`, `-617778`) — these are templated HVAC service pages with city-name swaps and boilerplate body content, exactly the target of the update. Uniform 10–40 position drops across 8 keywords in a 25-day window is algorithmic, not listing-level noise.
2. **Logo asset change with dual-brand alt text** — compounds the above by introducing entity ambiguity during a re-evaluation window. The new logo's alt attribute includes "now part of ... a larger Coastal Air Plus," which Google reads as content and mixes into the CCC entity graph.
3. **GBP signal disruption** — any edit to GBP NAP, categories, hours, photos, or ownership in the window can trigger a temporary local ranking dip. Dashboard audit log will confirm.
4. **WhatConverts tracking-number churn** — if any WhatConverts number published on the GBP was rotated, expired, or reassigned mid-window, that would dampen the Local Pack recovery independent of the organic impact.
5. **Citation NAP issues** — Yelp has "Lipton St" typo instead of "Lipman St" and two duplicate listings; baseline local-trust drag rather than the trigger itself.
6. **Competitor aggression / seasonal shift** — low probability given the uniformity of the drop and the algo-update timing match.

**Read of the compound effect:** Information Gain pulled the templated service pages down the organic SERP. GBP + logo + NAP issues then prevented the Local Pack from compensating. Two independent signal systems weakened in the same window — which is why the decline looks uniform across keywords.

**Action before touching this tier:** run the diagnostic in §7.1 to confirm the attribution split between the algo update (probable primary) and local signals (probable compounding factor). The content rewrites required by Information Gain recovery are in §11; they run in parallel with the local-signal diagnostic.

### Pillar 4 — AEO + Information Gain (novel, entity-dense, first-party content)

Google's Information Gain algorithm update (late March 2026) and the AEO / AI Overview opportunity reward the same signals: novel information, entity density, first-party data, and named authorship. Pillar 4 addresses both simultaneously — Information Gain defense is the *recovery* side (restoring rankings on the declining templated pages), and AEO optimization is the *offense* side (capturing AI Overview citations and assistant responses).

**What both systems reward:**
- Novel first-party data competitors can't copy (year-8 salt-air corrosion threshold east of I-26, April call-volume spike from pollen + humidity, Charleston peninsula vs. inland lifespan differential)
- Named first-party experience (specific Cane Bay builder-grade air handler patterns, Historic Summerville retrofit ductwork realities, Nexton short-cycling frequencies)
- Unique synthesis (connecting "Summerville Sneeze" pollen timing to HVAC IAQ load in April)
- Named authors with verifiable credentials (Derrick Hall byline, NATE certification, license #M111694)
- Concrete numbers, cited sources, and entity-dense sentences

**What both systems penalize:**
- Templated pages that restate generic HVAC content with a city-name swap (the `-617782`/`-617779` pattern pages that are currently declining)
- Boilerplate "what is AC repair?" openers, "why choose us?" sections restating industry norms
- FAQs that match the top 3 competitor FAQs verbatim
- Unsourced superlatives ("most trusted," "the best")

8+ tracked keywords now show AI Overviews on the Charleston SERP. Competitors are not yet optimizing for this structurally. The play:

**Content structure per page:**
1. **Direct answer block in the first 80–120 words.** One paragraph that answers the page-title question in plain sentences. No fluff lead-in, no credential parade. ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google's SGE will lift this block verbatim if it's clean.
2. **Entity-dense supporting section.** Named parts (capacitor, compressor, condenser, evaporator), named brands (Trane, service names), named places (Charleston, Summerville, Mount Pleasant neighborhoods). Entities get cited; adjectives don't.
3. **FAQ block with schema.** Every page carries 5–8 FAQs marked up with `FAQPage` schema. Questions pulled from People Also Ask and from the real call-center language in `icp.md`.
4. **HowTo schema on emergency spokes.** "AC blowing warm air — steps to try before calling" with `HowTo` schema. Even though the goal is to get them to call, the schema makes the page AEO-eligible.
5. **LocalBusiness schema on every location page.** `LocalBusiness` with `areaServed`, `geo`, `openingHours`, `priceRange`, `aggregateRating` pulled from Google reviews via a plugin or manual maintenance. `name` and `address` match the CCC Summerville GBP exactly.
6. **Organization schema site-wide** with `sameAs` pointing to every CCC brand profile in `assets.md` — YouTube, Facebook, Trane dealer, BBB, Yelp. Builds the knowledge graph entity for Coastal Carolina Comfort.

**Content topics specifically engineered for AI Overview capture:**
- "How much does AC repair cost in Charleston?" — answer with specific Charleston-relevant ranges; AI Overviews love concrete dollar figures.
- "How long do AC units last in the Lowcountry?" — this is where the salt-air-corrosion angle from positioning.md earns its keep.
- "Should I repair or replace my AC in Summerville?" — decision-tree content beats feature content for AEO.
- "What's the best HVAC company in Charleston?" — risky to answer about yourself; safer to explain the criteria a homeowner should use (which happen to be the criteria CCC meets).

**AEO tracking setup:**
- Monitor ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews for: "HVAC company Charleston SC," "AC repair Summerville," "best HVAC in Lowcountry," "crawl space encapsulation Charleston."
- Tool recommendation: AthenaHQ, Profound, or manual monthly audit via branded prompt tests.
- Track share-of-citation across 20–30 prompts monthly.

## 7. Diagnostics to run in week 1

### 7.1 Pre-work: diagnose the March–April decline

Before writing a single new page, run this diagnostic. The answers change which pillars move first.

1. **Summerville GBP ownership and audit log**
   - Who currently owns the listing? Any owner/manager changes since 2026-02-01?
   - Were primary category, hours, NAP, services, or photos edited between 2026-03-01 and 2026-04-23? Pull the full audit log.
   - Was the listing ever suspended, even briefly?
   - Was any review-reply burst, bulk photo upload, or attribute edit performed in mid-March? Bulk activity can trigger quality reviews.

2. **NAP audit (CCC entity only)**
   - Run BrightLocal or Yext citation audit on "Coastal Carolina Comfort, 110 Lipman St, Summerville, SC 29483" + the public CCC phone number.
   - Flag every listing with inconsistent NAP — especially any directory that was silently updated to a different phone, address, or brand name.

3. **Review velocity**
   - Count new Google reviews on the Summerville GBP month-by-month for the last 12 months. If review velocity dropped in March–April, that's an input to the ranking drop.

4. **Backlink profile check**
   - Ahrefs/Semrush backlink audit on coastalcarolinahvac.com. Any lost links in the last 60 days? Any new toxic links? Any anchor-text shift?

5. **Search Console query-level check**
   - Log into GSC for coastalcarolinahvac.com. Pull the same tracked keywords. Confirm the decline shows in impressions + average position, not just a tool-side reporting artifact.
   - Look at Coverage / Page indexing for any pages that dropped out of the index entirely.

6. **Site changes audit**
   - Were there CMS changes, theme updates, plugin changes, or template changes between 2026-03-01 and 2026-04-23? The logo swap is confirmed — was anything else touched (header, footer, schema, robots.txt, sitemap)? Confirm no accidental noindex tags or broken redirects.

7. **Core Web Vitals**
   - Run PageSpeed Insights on the top 5 ranking URLs. The `-617778/` and `-617779/` pattern URLs look like auto-generated template pages — verify they meet CWV thresholds.

Only after this diagnostic completes should any on-page or content work beyond the cannibalization consolidation start. If the cause is GBP or citation drift, the ranking recovery comes from fixing that, not from rewriting pages.
