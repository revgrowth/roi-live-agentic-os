# Coastal Air Plus — Ideal Customer Profile

## Primary ICP

**Established homeowner, age 35–65, in the Summerville/Charleston metro or Myrtle Beach corridor, owning their home outright or deep into a mortgage, with an HVAC system 10–20 years old that's starting to show it.**

The climate doesn't make HVAC optional in the Lowcountry. Summers are brutal, humidity is relentless, and coastal salt air corrodes outdoor units faster than inland. For this base, speed-to-onsite and trust beat lowest price almost every time.

## Two dominant lead segments

### Segment 1: Emergency Repair

**The scenario:** System dies mid-July. Humidity is 90%. Someone at the house is pregnant, elderly, or has a toddler napping upstairs. They Googled "AC not blowing cold" for five minutes, tried flipping the breaker, and now they're calling.

**Profile:**
- Mostly older homes (pre-2000) with original or first-replacement systems
- Speed and trust matter more than price
- Converts on phone calls far more than form submissions
- 60%+ have already tried a DIY diagnostic before calling
- Typical ticket: repair (if salvageable) or emergency replacement (if the system is end-of-life)

**What they need to hear:**
- A real person answering
- A real time commitment ("tech on the way, here by 4 PM")
- Something to do in the meantime (breaker, thermostat, what to watch for)
- Reassurance without dismissiveness — their day is genuinely ruined

**What kills the conversion:**
- IVR/phone tree
- Form-submission delay
- Hedged language ("we'll try to get someone out")
- Upsell pressure in the panic moment

### Segment 2: Planned Replacement & Maintenance

**The scenario:** Homeowner has had a bad experience with a cheap contractor, or has lived in the house long enough to know the system is on borrowed time. They're not panicked. They're researching.

**Profile:**
- Research-heavy: will read reviews, collect 2–3 quotes, ask specific questions
- Higher ticket (full system replacement, often with financing)
- Slower decision cycle (2–6 weeks)
- Values: warranty length, company tenure, tech qualifications, response time guarantees
- Concern: "Will this company still exist in 5 years to honor the warranty?"

**What they need to hear:**
- Specific warranty structure (parts, labor, compressor)
- Company history (the 1947 heritage earns its keep here, once verified)
- NATE-certified tech detail
- Transparent financing options
- Named proof — reviews, BBB, Trane dealer status
- "No high-pressure sales" messaging

**What kills the conversion:**
- Urgency manufactured where none exists
- Vague price ranges
- Unanswered warranty questions
- Obvious script-reading from the estimator

## Secondary segments

### Retirees / snowbirds with second homes (Myrtle Beach corridor)
- Arrive in March, don't want a dead system
- Convert well on maintenance-plan messaging
- Value remote communication and peace of mind
- Price-sensitive but not cheap — value predictability over upfront savings

### New-build homeowners in Cane Bay / Nexton / Carnes Crossroads
- Builder-grade systems hitting year 8–12 and starting to fail
- Often first-time dealing with major HVAC repair
- Higher-income, less HVAC-savvy
- Value clear explanations, warranty education, and financing options
- Susceptible to franchise upsell — Coastal wins here by being the local expert who explains instead of sells

## What they have in common

- **Homeowners, not renters.** Skin in the game.
- **Local and staying.** Not speculators, not short-term flips.
- **Trust is the currency.** The bad-contractor experience is common in this market — every segment is watching for it.
- **Phone > form** for urgent matters. Form submissions skew toward planned replacement.
- **Reviews matter.** Most will check at least BBB or Google before calling.

## Pain points (ranked)

1. System death during peak heat/humidity — existential, not an inconvenience
2. Past bad contractor experience (overpriced, no-show, incomplete work, no warranty honored)
3. Fear the company won't be around to warranty the install in 5 years
4. Salt-air corrosion cutting system life short (specific to coastal homes)
5. High replacement cost with unclear financing options
6. Rising utility bills from an aging inefficient system
7. Humidity and IAQ issues (Summerville Sneeze, pollen season, coastal mold)

## Channels that convert

- **Phone** — especially for emergency segment
- **Google local + Google Business Profile** — strong intent
- **Referrals** — heritage helps compound this
- **Facebook** — especially in Nexton/Cane Bay homeowner groups
- **Direct mail / door hangers in established neighborhoods** (Historic Summerville, Mount Pleasant)
- **Reviews** — BBB, Google, Yelp — do pre-conversion work

## Channels that under-perform (for this base)

- TikTok, Instagram Reels — wrong demographic for conversion, fine for awareness
- YouTube long-form — low intent match unless paired with Google search
- Twitter/X — essentially zero signal in this audience
- Podcasts — trust builder if the host is right; not a lead channel

## Notes for writing

- Speak to the homeowner's actual day, not their "home comfort needs"
- Reference the real neighborhoods by name — it's the single biggest trust signal for local intent
- Acknowledge past bad-contractor experiences directly rather than positioning as generic alternative
- Weather-specific detail (salt air, humidity numbers, pollen timing) does more trust-building than generic "we know the climate"
