# Coastal Air Plus — Positioning

## Primary angle

**The 78-year Lowcountry local, now with regional backing.**

Coastal Air Plus is the local HVAC operation a Summerville or Charleston homeowner would have chosen in 1978 — still run by people who answer the phone, still staffed by techs who know the specific quirks of pre-2000 Lowcountry housing stock and salt-air corrosion. What changed in 2026 is the infrastructure behind them: deeper inventory, stronger warranty backing, in-house plumbing capability, and the resources of a company that has weathered nearly eight decades of South Carolina summers.

Same people. Same phone number. More to stand behind the work.

## One-sentence positioning statement

> For established Lowcountry homeowners deciding between a national franchise (ARS, One Hour) and a small local shop, Coastal Air Plus is the long-tenured, fully licensed local operator that answers the phone, shows up fast, and has the operational depth to honor a warranty five years from now.

## Why this angle (and not others)

### Ruled out: "Cheapest price"
- Not the truth (they're not) and not what the customer wants (trust + speed beats price in both ICP segments).
- Cheapest is the Craigslist-contractor frame — the exact thing the "planned replacement" segment is trying to escape.

### Ruled out: "Latest technology / smart home leader"
- Doesn't match a 78-year heritage story.
- Trane Certified Dealer handles the tech credibility without forcing it into the hero position.

### Ruled out: "Since 1947" as the primary hook
- Heritage is proof, not promise. Leading with it reads like the About page, not a reason to call today.
- Plus the 1947 claim needs verification (see flags below) before it can anchor a campaign.

### Why "local, long-tenured, regionally backed" wins
- **Against national franchises** (ARS, One Hour): they can't credibly claim local ownership or long tenure — their whole model is rotating franchisees and corporate call centers. The contrast is sharpest here.
- **Against small local shops and Craigslist operators:** Coastal Air Plus has operational depth they can't match — licensure, in-house plumbing, financing, structured membership program, warranty infrastructure.
- **Against the acquired Coastal Carolina Comfort customer's fear:** the "now backed by a larger operation" framing addresses the real worry — "did my local company just disappear?" — without triggering the "corporate takeover" alarm.

## Competitive landscape (Summerville / Charleston)

### National franchises in market
- **ARS / Rescue Rooter** — national franchise, strongest marketing presence, weakest local ownership story
- **One Hour Heating & Air Conditioning** — franchise model, "always on time or it's free" tagline
- **Service Experts** — franchise, price-anchored

**Edge against all three:** local ownership, licensed SC mechanical contractor, longer tenure, in-house team (no franchisee variance).

### Local operators in market
- Mid-sized regional HVAC shops across Dorchester and Berkeley counties (several)
- Independent techs / Craigslist operators at the low-price end

**Edge against mid-sized shops:** operational depth (in-house plumbing, financing, membership program, warranty infrastructure).
**Edge against Craigslist tier:** licensure, insurance, reviews, warranty.

## Messaging guardrails

- **Lead with the homeowner's situation, not the company's credentials.** Credentials go in the proof section.
- **Never claim "the best" or "the top" without a specific receipt.** Use BBB rating, NATE certifications, specific warranty lengths, or review counts with dates.
- **Don't over-play the acquisition.** Most Summerville customers don't need the M&A story — they need to know their guy is still their guy. The regional-backing message is for planned-replacement research customers, not for everyday service pages.

Confirm ownership-continuity narrative before using "since 1947" in hero copy, press releases, or paid ad headlines.
2. **Warranty specifics.** What's the actual warranty structure on new installs? 10-year parts? Labor? Lifetime compressor? Need specifics to turn "stand behind the work" into a receipt.

4. **Membership program details.** Structure, price, what's included. Current copy mentions it but doesn't specify.
5. **CCC brand transition timeline.** When does the CCC site retire? When do CCC-branded trucks transition? Customer-facing comms plan?
