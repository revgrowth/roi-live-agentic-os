# Coastal Air Plus — Voice Profile

## One-line voice summary

The neighborhood expert who's been fixing ACs through 78 Lowcountry summers — warm enough to answer the panic call, sharp enough to explain why the salt air east of I-26 is killing your condenser coil.

## Baseline register

**Warm + Authoritative.** The tone of a long-tenured local HVAC company that knows Summerville, Mount Pleasant, and the Grand Strand by heart. Comfortable with technical specifics, but translates them into homeowner language. Confident without being cold. Never preaches; never over-sells the heritage.

### What that sounds like in practice

> "Most condenser coils east of I-26 show corrosion damage by year 8 because of salt air. If yours is approaching that age, a seasonal check catches the small stuff before it becomes a full replacement."

Notice what's happening:
- Specific geography ("east of I-26") — not generic "coastal areas"
- Technical claim with a number ("year 8," "corrosion damage")
- Ends on the homeowner's decision, not the sales pitch
- No exclamation, no urgency manufactured — the facts carry the weight

## Emergency register

**Reassuring + Fast.** When it's 95 degrees and the system is dead, the voice calms the panic without dismissing it and moves straight to what happens next. Calm confidence. No theatrics.

### What that sounds like in practice

> "Dead system and 95 degrees outside is the worst day of the summer. A tech is on the way. Here's what to do with your breaker and thermostat while we head over."

The pattern:
1. Acknowledge the moment (one line, not three)
2. State what we're doing
3. Give them something useful while they wait

## Words and phrases to use

### Geography specifics (always preferred over generic "Lowcountry" or "Charleston area")
- Summerville, Nexton, Cane Bay, Carnes Crossroads, Historic Summerville
- Mount Pleasant, West Ashley, James Island, Daniel Island
- Goose Creek, North Charleston
- Myrtle Beach corridor, Murrells Inlet, Pawleys Island
- "East of I-26" as a salt-air shorthand
- Berkeley, Dorchester, and Colleton counties

### Voice markers from the brand
- "Comfort you can count on from neighbors you trust" (CCC — anchor line)
- "The Lowcountry" (always with the article, capital L)
- "Rooted in Carolina soil"
- "No high-pressure sales"
- "Free in-home estimates"
- "Rest Easy Guarantee"
- "NATE Certified technicians"
- "Same local team, deeper resources" (for integration messaging)

### Local weather vocabulary (use these, not generic climate talk)
- Lowcountry summers (not "hot summers")
- Humidity (specifically 90%+ in July/August — a number, not an adjective)
- Salt air corrosion (the real competitive differentiator on coil life)
- Pollen season / "Summerville Sneeze" / Flowertown (for filtration and IAQ content)

## Words and phrases to avoid

### Generic HVAC franchise language
- "Your trusted partner in comfort"
- "South Carolina's preferred choice"
- "We're not happy until you're happy"
- "Dedicated to excellence"
- Any variant of "world-class service"

### Tone misfires
- **Playful / edgy / punny.** Doesn't fit a 78-year-old company or the 35–65 homeowner. No "beating the heat" wordplay, no AC jokes.
- **Dispatcher-script corporate.** The feel of a call being routed through a national center is exactly what franchises (ARS, One Hour) sound like. That's the edge we're exploiting — don't forfeit it.
- **Over-levered heritage.** "Since 1947" is context, not a headline repeated on every page. Use it once per page max, usually in a subhead or proof section, never in the H1 unless the page is specifically about company history.

### Hedges that weaken the authority
- "We strive to..." → "We do."
- "We believe that..." → State the thing.
- "One of the best..." → Say the specific thing that's true.

## Sentence rhythm

- Mix short and mid-length. Avoid long compound sentences — homeowners scan.
- Start sentences with the homeowner's world, not the company's. *"When your system dies mid-July..."* beats *"Coastal Air Plus offers emergency repair..."*
- Use the specific over the abstract. "Coil corrosion by year 8" beats "systems wear out."

## Handling the two-brand transition

Coastal Carolina Comfort was acquired 2026-03-31 and is in Phase 1 of brand transition (site still live). When writing for the Summerville/Charleston market:
- Lean on CCC's voice as the primary anchor — it already sounds local
- Reference the parent brand for heritage and resource depth, not day-to-day trust
- Use "same local team, deeper resources" framing rather than "we acquired them"
- Never imply the customer's technician or office contact is changing unless confirmed
